## 3. What the reference sites add

The reference sites are useful in three specific ways:

1. **Coverage and taxonomy**
   - The source corpus is materially broader than Blisse's current position library.
   - Even from the curated landing-page samples alone, there are many named variants not currently present in Blisse.

2. **Cross-language naming patterns**
   - The EN / ES / PT pages make it easier to compare how the same concept is labeled across languages.

3. **Discovery of missing setup-based variants**
   - Especially for furniture-based positions, angle-based variants, and oral-position setups.

Important caution:
- The reference sites should be used as a **catalog and naming reference**, not as a copy source.
- Their translations are inconsistent in places and are not polished enough to use verbatim.
- Their visual/content tone also pushes beyond the current Blisse App Store safety ceiling in places.

1. underrepresented sub-variants within strong categories
2. missing named variants users expect to find from broader public catalogs
3. weaker setup specificity for certain positions
4. limited hidden tags for discoverability

## 8. Localization and naturalization audit

### The good news
The ES and PT content libraries are fully populated.
There are no missing overlay fields in the generated catalogs.

### The actual issue
Some copy still feels:
- partially literal
- partially English-influenced
- occasionally brand-like rather than native

### Spanish observations
Spanish is generally in decent shape, but some lines still feel slightly stiff or over-literal.
Examples of patterns to smooth:
- instructional imperatives that sound translated rather than conversational
- repetition of phrasing like `quédate aquí` in contexts where a more natural phrasing would read better
- some title choices feel serviceable rather than native and sexy/playful

### Portuguese observations
Portuguese needs more naturalization than Spanish.
Examples already observed in current content:
- `Breath Play (leve)`
- `Música Playlist`
- `Roleplay Roleta`
- `A contagem regressiva Touch`

These are understandable, but not truly native.

### Reference-site translation caution
The PT and ES source sites contain many naming artifacts that should **not** be copied literally.
Examples:
- `Montaña creek`
- `Missionário (Papai e Mamãe)`
- `Rebocador de guerra`
- `Ladyboy`
- inconsistent numbering prefixes in titles

Conclusion:
- use the reference sites to identify concepts and naming families
- do not adopt their prose as the final localized copy

## 11. Bottom line

Blisse is already content-rich enough to feel substantial.
The priority is not random volume. The priority is:
- **smarter depth** in positions
- **setup variety** in oral play
- **better natural-language polish** in ES and especially PT
- **secondary tags** for better discovery and personalization

The reference sites are genuinely useful, but best used as:
- a gap map
- a taxonomy reference
- a cross-language naming corpus

They should not be used as copy-ready localized content.
